Welcome

The Notre Dame Basses are the proud section of the marching band that plays the biggest, loudest, and best instrument (the sousaphone) in The Band of the Fighting Irish.

Traditions

Bass traditions include singing school songs near Corby Hall after the Concert on the Step, seniors performing a Bass Talk about the opposing team before marching into the stadium, and wearing HANK suits for Friday marchouts before Gameday.

Time Commitment

Marching Band practices are Monday-Thursday from 6:30pm-8:00pm. There are Friday marchouts (the day before Game Day) from 4:20-6pm.

Schedule on Football Game Day

  • Morning marchout around the campus to wake up the school
  • Practice in the indoor Loftus Practice Field
  • Break for Lunch
  • Concert on the steps of Bond Hall
  • Basses walk over to Corby Hall and sing school songs
  • Band marches out from the Main Building to the Stadium
  • During the break before the band marches onto the field a senior bass does a Bass Talk about the opposing team
  • Band performs Pregame performance
  • Band plays songs during football game from the sidelines
  • Band performs the Halftime Show
  • After the game the Band performs the Postgame performance
  • Band marches back to the Band building
  • Finished!

How Do I Join?

You must be a Holy Cross, St. Mary's, or Notre Dame student to audition for the Band of the Fighting Irish. Band camp begins the Wednesday before the first day of school and you are allowed to move into your dorm the day before band camp. Auditions include a separate music and marching audition for the directors. Click here for more information on the Notre Dame Band website.
